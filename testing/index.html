Microtubule Self-Assembly Simulation

Investigate the effects of tubulin protein concentration on microtuble self-assembly kinetics in silico.

Microtubule Self-Assembly Assay:

Tubulin protein in solution is heated to 37Â° C . Heat energy increases the thermal motion of the tubulin molecules, causing them to collide with one another and assemble into microtubules. Microtubule assembly is measured as an increase in the optical density of the solution, as the rod-like shape of microtubules causes the scattering of 340 nm light.

Experimental Variable Input:

Use the slider to adjust the concentration of tubulin protein used in the microtubule self-assembly reaction.
